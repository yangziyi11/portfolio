OVERVIEW

During my 1 year internship at GE appliances (2016 summer -2017 summer), I helped to provide several high-fidelity prototypes to support demonstration purpose and user testing purpose.

Tools I used is Framer.js, and sometimes Hype as well. Compared to Invision, Framer adn Hype are easier to build more complex prototypes, which is closer to the real product. I listed some of my works below.

Minimal Kitchen

To prepare for one of the Appliances show in 2016 summer, I was working in a team of 7 designers (4 industrial designers + 3 UX designers), to build a conceptual design of minimal kitchen, with the high-end Monogram line style.

The minimal kitchen consists of cooktop, fridge, oven, range hood and everything a kitchen needs.The hub of it is the Fridge screen. Users can check whether/ fridge/ available storage with it. Based on the design released by other 3 designers, I prototyped the hub control system and it was preloaded in a iPad as a fake screen inside of the minimal kitchen physical prototype. The Director for the whole design department like this prototype :)

Even the clock is fully functional - It reads the system time.

GE Laundry

This prototype is made according to the GE laundry App UX guideline. We made this prototype to showcase the newly added schedule feature and to get feedback for the whole flow before the actual application udpate got implemented.

Something special for this prototype is that not only our human factor team uses it for controlled user testing, but our salesman also used it to demonstrate the compatible washer and dryer to real consumers to get some broader feedback. For this reason, we need to make this prototype really high fidelity, and compatible to different size of phones.

When run this prototype on phone, testers can save it (as a webpage) to the phone's home screen. After doing it, it even shows the real app icon.

GE Comfort

I made this prototype to showcase the newly added schedule feature and modified interface for setting target temperature. To help our human factor team to gather accurate user feedback, the diagram reacts to the modified schedule quite accurately. Unfortunately, due to the time limit, we only allow testers/users to modify "+add new schedule" once.
